Case Study
The Capital One Breach Explained

July 2019 — A misconfigured AWS WAF allowed an attacker to perform a Server Side Request Forgery (SSRF) attack, accessing the EC2 metadata service and stealing temporary credentials. Over 100 million customer records were exposed.

Lesson: Restrict the EC2 metadata service (IMDSv2) and audit WAF rules regularly.

Guide
Why S3 Buckets Get Exposed

Public S3 buckets are one of the most common cloud misconfigurations. They often happen when developers set buckets to public during testing and forget to revert the setting before deploying to production.

Fix: Enable "Block all public access" at the account level in AWS, not just per bucket.

Case Study
Uber's 2022 Social Engineering Attack

An attacker texted an Uber employee posing as IT support, convinced them to share their VPN credentials, then accessed internal Slack, HackerOne reports, and cloud infrastructure. No malware was used — just manipulation.

Lesson: Train staff to verify identity requests. MFA alone isn't enough without user awareness.
